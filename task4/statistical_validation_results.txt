=== STATISTICAL VALIDATION & HYPOTHESIS TESTING RESULTS ===

### Two-Sample Independent Welch's T-Test
Hypothesis:
- Null Hypothesis (H0): Mean Weekday Spend = Mean Weekend Spend
- Alternative Hypothesis (H1): Mean Weekday Spend != Mean Weekend Spend

Summary Statistics:
- Weekdays: n = 712, mean = ₹141893.71, std = ₹114380.54
- Weekends: n = 288, mean = ₹133233.06, std = ₹113365.90

Test Results:
- t-statistic: 1.0911
- p-value: 0.2757
- Degrees of Freedom: 535.37
- Mean Difference: ₹8660.65
- 95% Confidence Interval of Difference: [₹-6931.24, ₹24252.54]

Conclusion: The difference in average transaction values between weekdays and weekends is NOT STATISTICALLY SIGNIFICANT (alpha = 0.05).
We fail to reject the null hypothesis H0. There is no statistical evidence that weekday and weekend transactions generate different mean spends.
